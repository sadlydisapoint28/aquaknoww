Freshwater Fish

Fish that live in freshwater environments like rivers, lakes, and ponds.

Examples:

  • Tilapia - Popular aquaculture fish, native to Africa
  • Catfish (Hito) - Bottom-dwelling fish with whisker-like barbels
  • Dalag (Snakehead) - Predatory fish that can breathe air

ğŸ” Did You Know?

Freshwater fish have adapted to live in water with low salt content. The Philippines has over 2,400 freshwater fish species, with many endemic to specific islands like Lake Lanao's 18 endemic cyprinid species!

Marine Fish

Fish that live in saltwater environments like oceans and seas.

Examples:

  • Tuna - Fast-swimming pelagic fish, highly valued commercially
  • Lapu-Lapu (Grouper) - Reef fish prized for its taste
  • Mackerel - Nutrient-rich fish with high omega-3 content
  • Dilis (Anchovies) - Small schooling fish, important food source

ğŸ” Did You Know?

Marine fish have special adaptations to handle saltwater. The Philippines, being an archipelago, has incredibly rich marine biodiversity with over 2,000 marine fish species - that's about 10% of all known fish species worldwide!

Anadromous Fish

Fish that migrate between fresh and saltwater during their life cycle.

Examples:

  • Salmon - Born in freshwater, mature in ocean, return to spawn
  • Milkfish (Bangus) - Can adapt to both fresh and saltwater

ğŸ” Did You Know?

Anadromous fish have remarkable homing abilities, often returning to the exact stream where they were born to reproduce, sometimes traveling thousands of miles!

Gills & Breathing

Fish extract oxygen from water through their gills. Water flows over gill filaments where oxygen is absorbed into the bloodstream and carbon dioxide is released.

Fins & Movement

Different fins serve various purposes: dorsal fins provide stability, pectoral fins help with steering, and the caudal (tail) fin provides propulsion.

Why Fish Conservation Matters

Fish populations worldwide face threats from overfishing, habitat destruction, pollution, and climate change. Understanding these challenges helps us make informed choices about seafood consumption and support conservation efforts.

ğŸ£ Sustainable Fishing Practices

  • Choose fish from well-managed fisheries
  • Support local, small-scale fishing operations
  • Avoid fish during their breeding seasons
  • Practice catch and release when recreational fishing
  • Use appropriate fishing gear to minimize bycatch
